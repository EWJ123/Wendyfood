Satay

Satay, also call as sate. It's a popular Malaysian delicacy skewered and grilled to smoky perfection. Thinly sliced morsels of chicken, beef, or tender lamb immersed in an exotic blend of spices before cooking over scorching coals. Aromas waft while the meat takes on a tender texture. Traditionally, these kabobs come served with a rich peanut sauce for dipping, rice cakes for soaking up flavor, and refreshing slices of cucumber and onion. Loved for its complexity of savory, subtly sweet and warming spicy notes, satay satisfies both hunger and palate at lively night markets and food stalls under the stars.

Beyond Malaysia, satay has spread throughout Southeast Asia and beyond. Each region adds its own twist to the dish. From Indonesian sate to Thai satay, the core of skewered, spiced, and grilled meat stays consistent. This makes it a unifying symbol of Southeast Asian street food culture, continuing to attract fans worldwide.
