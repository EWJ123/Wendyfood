Takoyaki

Takoyaki is a popular Japanese street food. It's light crispy batter is stuffed with diced octopus pickled ginger and chopped green onions. It's golden brown and crispy on the outside but soft and steamy inside, the final touch is usually a soft savory sauce, a dab of mayonnaise and bonito flakes that wriggle in the heat. Takoyaki makes a snack unlike any other available food. It is rich in flavour yet a bit chewy. Takoyaki is said to have first been eaten at Osakas Festival, night markets and streetfood stalls.

Today, takoyaki is one of Osaka's signature dishes and a symbol of Japanese festival culture. You can find it at yatai (food stalls), shopping streets, and even specialty restaurants. It has become popular beyond Japan, delighting people around the world with its unique mix of crispy, creamy, and savory flavors
